nara

Data Scientist

Center for Molecular Medicine (CeMM)

In the complex network of interconnected branches of Science pushing the frontiers of human knowledge, I am positioned at the interface of three vertices 1. Biomedicine 2. High-throughput Technology and 3. Informatics. Naturally, my academic interests span a wide range of thema that are connected to the vertices. Over the years, I gained specific expertise in Development and Application of Computational Methods - from Data Management to Exploratory Visualization - as a means to address real world Biomedical challenges.

For example, at Institute for Plant Genetics and Crop Plant Research, I addressed two research themes 1. Elucidate transcript, protein and metabolite level changes accompanying root senescence and 2. Identify gene expression patterns in nutrient perturbed crop plants.

At Leibniz Lung Center, the challenge was to develop computational methods for mass spectrometry data. 1. I developed an error modeling technique 2. Independently designed, built and implemented a novel metric for determining lipidome homology. At the National Center for Biological Sciences, I applied sequence alignment algorithms for small molecule structure comparison and developed a stochastic model of Transcription. At the Center for Cellular and Molecular Biology, the focus was to reconstruct microbial metabolic pathways from publicly available databases and analyze the network topology.
